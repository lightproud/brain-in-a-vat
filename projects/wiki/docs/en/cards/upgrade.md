# Card Upgrades

Every card in Morimens can be upgraded up to **6 times**, progressively increasing its power, modifying its effects, or reducing its Arithmetica cost.

## Upgrade Levels

| Level | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| Base (Lv 0) | Default card stats and effects |
| Lv 1 | Minor stat increase |
| Lv 2 | Stat increase or minor effect enhancement |
| Lv 3 | Notable power spike; may add secondary effects |
| Lv 4 | Significant enhancement |
| Lv 5 | Major power increase |
| Lv 6 (Max) | Maximum power; may transform the card's effect or add a new mechanic |

::: tip
Upgrade Level 3 and Level 6 are common breakpoints where cards gain qualitative improvements (new effects) rather than just numerical buffs. Prioritize reaching these thresholds.
:::

## Upgrade Materials

Card upgrades require materials obtained from:

- [Resource Stages](../stages/resource.md)
- [Investigation](../modes/investigation.md) rewards
- Event shops
- [Smelting Room](../modes/smelting.md) crafting

*Specific material names and quantities to be added.*

## Upgrade Priority

1. **Main DPS Command Cards** — Maximize your primary damage dealer's output first.
2. **Spirit Awakening Cards** — High-impact abilities benefit greatly from upgrades.
3. **Support utility cards** — Upgraded heals and buffs scale your whole team.
4. **Tank defensive cards** — Lower priority but valuable for hard content.

::: warning
Card upgrade materials are limited, especially early on. Do not spread upgrades evenly across all cards. Focus on your core team's most impactful cards first.
:::

## How Upgrades Affect Deckbuilding

Upgraded cards can change the optimal way to play a deck. A card that is mediocre at Lv 0 may become a core combo piece at Lv 6. Re-evaluate your [deck composition](./deckbuilding.md) as you upgrade key cards.
