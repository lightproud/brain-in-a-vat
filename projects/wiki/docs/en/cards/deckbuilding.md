# Deckbuilding Guide

Effective deckbuilding in Morimens means selecting the right Awakeners and understanding how their cards interact within a Designated Realm's mechanics.

## Core Principles

1. **Realm Cohesion** -- Build your deck around one primary realm to maximize synergy.
2. **Arithmetica Curve** -- Balance high-cost and low-cost cards so you can play efficiently each turn with your 5 Arithmetica budget.
3. **Win Condition** -- Identify how your deck wins (burst damage, sustained pressure, Silver Key activation, etc.) and build toward it.
4. **Madness Management** -- Factor in when Madness Burst will trigger and plan turns around the free ability.

## Deckbuilding by Realm

### <span class="realm-badge realm-chaos">Chaos</span> Decks

Chaos decks are the most flexible, able to mix cards from multiple realms.

- **Silver Key Focus** -- Chaos cards accelerate Silver Key generation. Build toward activating your [Key Order](../key-orders/index.md) as the primary win condition.
- **Hybrid Picks** -- Include the strongest individual cards from other realms without worrying about breaking synergy.
- **Versatility** -- Best realm for players who lack a full roster in a single realm.

::: tip
Chaos decks benefit from cards that generate Silver Key passively. Prioritize these over raw damage cards when building a Chaos-focused deck.
:::

### <span class="realm-badge realm-aequor">Aequor</span> Decks

Aequor decks revolve around summoning Tentacle entities and empowering them.

- **Summoning Engine** -- Include enough Tentacle-summoning cards to maintain board presence.
- **Tentacle Buffs** -- Pair summoning cards with cards that enhance Tentacle damage or grant Tentacles additional effects.
- **Sustained Damage** -- Tentacles persist across turns, providing continuous damage while you play support cards.

::: tip
A common mistake in Aequor deckbuilding is including too many summoning cards and not enough buff cards. Once you have 2-3 summoning sources, focus on empowerment.
:::

### <span class="realm-badge realm-caro">Caro</span> Decks

Caro decks create Embryo cards and consume them for powerful burst effects.

- **Generation and Consumption** -- Balance cards that create Embryos with cards that consume them. Too many generators with no consumers leads to a clogged hand.
- **Hand Management** -- Embryo cards take up hand space. Plan your turn order carefully.
- **Burst Windows** -- Caro decks excel at burst damage when consuming multiple Embryos in a single turn.

::: warning
Embryo cards cost Arithmetica to play. Account for this additional cost when planning your Arithmetica curve.
:::

### <span class="realm-badge realm-ultra">Ultra</span> Decks

Ultra decks gain extra turns from playing multiple cards, creating snowball potential.

- **Low-Cost Cards** -- Prioritize cards with low Arithmetica costs to play more cards per turn and trigger extra turn effects.
- **Chain Potential** -- Look for cards that draw additional cards or refund Arithmetica, extending your turn further.
- **Momentum** -- Ultra decks start slow but accelerate. Survive the early turns to reach your power spike.

::: tip
Ultra decks live or die by Arithmetica efficiency. A single high-cost card can break your chain. Keep your average card cost as low as possible.
:::

## General Deckbuilding Tips

- **Upgrade key cards first** -- A Lv 6 core card is worth more than several Lv 2 cards. See [Card Upgrades](./upgrade.md).
- **Test in Investigation** -- Use [Investigation](../modes/investigation.md) runs to experiment with card combinations before committing upgrade materials.
- **Adapt to content** -- Some stages favor certain realms or strategies. Do not be afraid to swap cards between runs.
- **Consider Spirit Awakening timing** -- Your Spirit Awakening Card is your strongest play. Build your deck to set up the conditions for maximum impact.
