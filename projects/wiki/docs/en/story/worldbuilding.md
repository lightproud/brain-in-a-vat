# Worldbuilding

Morimens is set in a world deeply influenced by the **Cthulhu Mythos** and Lovecraftian horror. This page catalogues the key concepts, factions, and lore elements of the game's world.

## Cthulhu Mythos Concepts

The game draws from and reinterprets classic Lovecraftian concepts:

| Game Concept | Mythos Inspiration | Role in Morimens |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Awakeners (唤醒体) | Awakened humans | Playable characters with eldritch powers |
| Designated Realms | Domains of cosmic entities | Four schools of power (Chaos, Aequor, Caro, Ultra) |
| Madness Points (狂气) | Sanity loss | Combat mechanic; triggers Madness Burst at 100 |
| Silver Key | The Silver Key (Lovecraft) | Combat energy; activates Key Orders at 1000 |
| The Keeper | Investigator / Curator | Player character; protector of Awakeners |
| Night of Oblivion (忘却前夜) | Cosmic apocalypse | The world-ending event the story revolves around |
| Investigation | Mythos investigation | Roguelite exploration mode |
| Tentacles (Aequor) | Deep Ones / Cthulhu | Summoned entities in combat |
| Embryos (Caro) | Shoggoths / flesh horror | Created and consumed cards |

## The World

> This world is dying. Facing a hidden and unspeakable calamity, Mythag University -- one of the few who know the truth -- has resolved to awaken a power that shares its origin with the disaster itself, forging bonds with human weapons who walk the edge of madness, to resist this vast crisis.

The world of Morimens is steeped in a **Victorian/Edwardian fog-London aesthetic**, blended with Lovecraftian cosmic horror. The core theme is **Memory and Forgetting** -- the tension between what we choose to remember and what the world forces us to lose.

> "You are no savior -- merely someone walking the edge of madness, stepping closer to the truth, one step at a time."

### The Night of Oblivion

The "Night of Oblivion" (忘却前夜) is the central catastrophe of Morimens' world. It is a cyclical event that threatens to erase memory, identity, and ultimately existence itself. The Keeper and the Awakeners stand against this tide.

### The Erosion

Hundreds of years ago, the **Erosion** (融蚀) silently descended upon the world. Life, sanity, memory -- everything that humanity imbues with "meaning" is gradually erased by the Erosion, leaving no trace. People remain utterly unaware of its existence. Those infected by the Erosion mutate into monsters and are completely erased from the world -- including all memories others have of them.

> "Welcome to Mythag University, Keeper. But there is nothing to congratulate -- you will be unknown, unrewarded, unremembered."

### Key Roles

| Role | Title | Description |
|------|-------|-------------|
| **The Keeper** (守密人) | Secret Keeper | A student/agent of Mythag University. Investigates the cause of lost memories, leading Awakeners into battle and assembling strategic card decks to uncover a twisted, maddening truth |
| **Awakened Ones** (唤醒体) | Awakeners | Human weapons walking the edge of madness, carriers of a power that shares its origin with the Erosion. Each Awakener has a unique personality, backstory, and personal story questline |

## The Four Realms as Cosmic Domains

Each Designated Realm represents a different facet of the unknowable:

- <span class="realm-badge realm-chaos">Chaos</span> -- The primordial void from which all things emerged. Represents formlessness and infinite potential.
- <span class="realm-badge realm-aequor">Aequor</span> -- The abyssal deep, where ancient entities slumber beneath impossible pressures.
- <span class="realm-badge realm-caro">Caro</span> -- The flesh that reshapes itself, evoking body horror and mutation beyond natural law.
- <span class="realm-badge realm-ultra">Ultra</span> -- The dimensions beyond human perception, where time and space lose meaning.

## Timeline

The main story spans approximately **800,000 characters of text**, told in an episodic unit-drama format across two major arcs:

### Oblivion Chapter (忘却篇)

Prologue through Chapter 7. The Keeper arrives at Londinium, bonds with the initial four Awakeners (Doll, Ramona, Lotan, Ogier), and uncovers the truth behind the Erosion.

| Chapter | Title | Summary |
|---------|-------|---------|
| Prologue | Prologue | The Keeper arrives at Londinium and begins their journey at Mythag University. Bonds with the starting four Awakeners and learns about the Erosion and the nature of Awakened Ones |
| 1 | East District Secrets | A layered investigation in the impoverished East District: from a workshop manager, to a suspicious doctor, to a cult. The truth centers on a girl controlled by dark forces who sacrifices herself to protect those she loves |
| 2 | Metamorphosis | The bonds between Keeper and Awakeners deepen. The Keeper witnesses the cruelty of Erosion-induced metamorphosis firsthand and begins to understand that Awakeners are not merely weapons |
| 3 | Chapter 3 | Multiple factions vie for power. The Sculptors' Association, Church of the Lightbearers, and others emerge, while Mythag University's true motives come under question |
| 4 | Chapter 4 | A major turning point. The Keeper brings rescued girl Lily to the Sanatorium, only to discover a greater crisis lurking within its walls |
| 5 | Chapter 5 | Multiple plot threads converge. Foreshadowing from earlier chapters pays off as faction plans collide |
| 6 | Chapter 6 | Factions show their true colors. The Keeper questions the cost of "protecting the truth." The hidden connection between Mythag University and the Erosion surfaces |
| 7 | Chapter 7 | The Oblivion Chapter finale. Includes boss stages like 7-18 "Kindling." The Keeper faces the ultimate test: forget the pain and find peace, or remember everything and carry the burden forward |

### Stellar Chapter (星辰篇)

A new arc following the Oblivion Chapter's conclusion. New cosmic forces emerge and the meaning of "awakening" deepens.

| Chapter | Title | Stages | Summary |
|---------|-------|--------|---------|
| Prologue | Stellar Prologue | -- | The world's landscape has shifted. New threats and allies appear simultaneously |
| 1 | Chapter 1 | 1-1 to 1-14 | Awakeners change under the influence of stellar power. The Keeper must re-understand their companions' true nature |
| 2 | Metamorphosis | 2-1 to 2-18 | Echoing the Oblivion Chapter's arc of the same name, but with deeper meaning -- stellar power brings inner awakening, not just outer change |
| 3 | Chapter 3 | 3-1 to 3-11 | Hidden truths within prophecies gradually surface. The guidance of the stars conceals a grander plan |
| 4 | Paradise | 4-0 to 4-16 | The chapter with the steepest difficulty curve. *"Why has the Black Sun eclipsed the sky?"* The Black Sun imagery pervades the entire chapter, culminating in an extremely challenging final boss |
