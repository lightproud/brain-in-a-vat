# Enlightenment

**Enlightenment** (启灵) is Morimens' duplicate constellation system. When you pull a duplicate of an Awakener you already own, the duplicate is converted into Enlightenment progress, unlocking powerful passive bonuses.

## How It Works

- Each duplicate copy of an Awakener grants **1 Enlightenment level**.
- Enlightenment has a maximum of **5 levels** per Awakener.
- Each level unlocks a unique passive bonus or stat increase specific to that Awakener.

## Enlightenment Levels

| Level | Duplicates Needed | Typical Bonus |
|-------|-------------------|---------------|
| 1 | 1 duplicate (2 total copies) | Minor stat boost or card enhancement |
| 2 | 2 duplicates (3 total copies) | Ability upgrade or new passive effect |
| 3 | 3 duplicates (4 total copies) | Significant power increase |
| 4 | 4 duplicates (5 total copies) | Major ability enhancement |
| 5 | 5 duplicates (6 total copies) | Maximum power; signature passive upgrade |

::: warning
Enlightenment 5 requires pulling 6 total copies of the same Awakener. For SSR characters, this is a significant investment. Plan your pulls accordingly.
:::

## Is Enlightenment Necessary?

For most content, Enlightenment is **not required**. Awakeners at Enlightenment 0 (no duplicates) are fully functional. Enlightenment levels provide incremental power boosts but are not gated behind any mandatory content.

::: tip
For free-to-play and low-spending players, it is generally better to diversify your roster across multiple Awakeners rather than chasing high Enlightenment levels on a single character.
:::

## Enlightenment by Rarity

- <span class="rarity-ssr">SSR</span> — Hardest to Enlighten due to low pull rate; each level is a substantial power gain
- <span class="rarity-sr">SR</span> — Moderate to Enlighten; duplicates accumulate over time
- <span class="rarity-r">R</span> — Easiest to Enlighten; often reach max Enlightenment through normal play
